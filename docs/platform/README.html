Platform migration

This repo becomes the world-wide-webb product platform.

The work is not a copy-paste migration. The end state is a platform-shaped monorepo where apps declare intent and the platform provides namespaces, routing, secrets, Postgres databases, backups, CI, deploy, local dev, and iOS workflow conventions.

What This Captures

Four Products

private captive public amp

control-center, captive-portal, text-your-ex, and amp are first-class products under one platform.

Platform Guarantees

Product namespaces, deploy, routing, TLS, secrets, Postgres, backups, and local workflows are paved roads, not bespoke app work.

Production Scope

The migration ends with all four products deployed to homelab k3s through product-aware CI and platform primitives.

Decisions Already Made

Repo identity: this repo will eventually be renamed to world-wide-webb.

Product layout: products live under products/<name>.

Product set: control-center, captive-portal, text-your-ex, and amp. Short labels are networking-only and derived from product names.

Hostnames: nested product hostnames are allowed, but TLS is explicit. *.worldwidewebb.co does not cover app.cc.worldwidewebb.co.

Database standard: per-product CNPG Postgres clusters, not SQLite or libSQL.

Backups: platform-managed, mandatory, backed up to the NAS, retained forever.

Cloud: target-aware design, but this migration ships homelab k3s only.

Done Means

  • All four products have product folders, product namespaces, product CI/deploy paths, and explicit service route declarations where they need external surfaces.
  • Control Center still works and is protected by the new private exposure model.
  • Captive Portal is its own product with redesigned API/backend/storage and preserved guest-network behavior.
  • Text Your Ex is deployed to production, with bundled iOS TestFlight flow pointing at its hosted API.
  • AMP is deployed as a minimal production management plane shell.
  • Every product database is backed up automatically and restore validation is part of the operational contract.
  • App number five is easy to add without copying low-level Kubernetes, Cloudflare, UniFi, secrets, backup, or iOS wiring.
